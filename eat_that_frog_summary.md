# Eat That Frog! Summary

## Core Principle
The core principle of "Eat That Frog!" is to tackle your most difficult and important task—your "frog"—first thing in the morning. By completing your most challenging task at the start of the day, you build momentum and prevent procrastination.

## Key Strategies

### 1. Identify Your Frog
- Your "frog" is the task you are most likely to procrastinate on, but which has the greatest impact on your goals.
- It is often the most difficult, most important, and most daunting task on your list.

### 2. Prepare the Night Before
- At the end of each day, review your tasks and identify the "frog" for the next day.
- This allows you to start working immediately in the morning without wasting time deciding what to do.

### 3. Eat the Frog First
- Do not start your day with small, easy tasks (like checking email).
- Dedicate your peak energy and focus to the biggest, hardest task.

### 4. Break Down Large Tasks
- If a task is too large and overwhelming, break it into smaller, more manageable "tadpoles."
- This makes it easier to begin and prevents the task from feeling insurmountable.

### 5. Use the 80/20 Principle
- Focus on the 20% of tasks that will yield 80% of your results.
- Prioritize tasks that align with your long-term professional and work goals.

## Application for IT Management at UW SPH
- **Priority Tasks:** System migrations, security audits, infrastructure upgrades, and strategic planning.
- **Frequent "Frogs":** Complex troubleshooting, difficult vendor negotiations, and large-scale project management.
- **Daily Implementation:** Start each day by selecting the most critical IT infrastructure or service issue to address before moving to administrative or routine maintenance tasks.
